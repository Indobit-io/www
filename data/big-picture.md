## Current Stance

The global liquidity cycle is in transition. The Fed has conducted significant
quantitative tightening since 2022, reducing the balance sheet from a peak near
$9T. Overnight reverse repo (ON RRP) has drained from a peak of ~$2.5T back
toward zero, signaling the excess liquidity buffer is exhausted.

## Key Tensions

1. **Fed vs. markets**: Rate cuts are priced in but the timeline keeps shifting
   as inflation proves sticky. Higher-for-longer weighs on risk assets.
2. **Dollar dynamics**: A strong DXY tightens global financial conditions
   outside the US, creating stress in EM markets and suppressing commodity prices.
3. **Cash on sidelines**: Money market funds hold record assets. This dry powder
   is waiting for clarity on rate path before rotating into risk assets.
4. **Crypto liquidity**: Stablecoin supply expansion is an early signal of
   capital entering the crypto ecosystem — watch this alongside BTC price action.

## Watch List

- RRP balance approaching zero → next liquidity drain hits bank reserves
- Fed balance sheet trajectory (QT pace vs. bank stress signals)
- 10Y yield — key level at 4.5% for risk asset pressure
- DXY — break below 100 would be meaningful dollar weakness signal
